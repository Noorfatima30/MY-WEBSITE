Favorite Personalitie

Imran Khan

Imran Khan is a Pakistani politician, former cricketer, and philanthropist who is currently serving as the Prime Minister of Pakistan. He was born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan. After retiring from cricket in 1992, he founded the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital and Research Centre. In 1996, he founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) political party, advocating for social and economic justice, anti-corruption measures, and a more independent foreign policy. After years of opposition, his PTI party won the 2018 general election , and he became Prime Minister on August 18, 2018.

Quaid-e-Azam

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was the founder and first Governor-General of Pakistan. He was born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, British India. Jinnah was a lawyer by profession and was a prominent leader in the Indian National Congress before he founded the Muslim League in 1906. He led the struggle for the creation of Pakistan, which became an independent nation in 1947. Jinnah is widely regarded as one of the most important figures in South Asian history, and his leadership played a crucial role in the creation of Pakistan. He passed away on September 11, 1948.

Allama Muhammad Iqbal

Allama Muhammad Iqbal was a poet, philosopher, and politician who is widely regarded as one of the most influential thinkers in the Indian subcontinent. He was born on November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, British India. Iqbal is best known for his poetry, which explored themes of spirituality, nationalism, and the Muslim identity. He played an instrumental role in the creation of Pakistan, and his philosophical writings have had a profound impact on modern Islamic thought. Iqbal passed away on April 21, 1938, but his legacy continues to inspire people around the world.
